DESIGN

BOOK JACKET MEMOIR

The purpose of the book jacket design was to entice a targeted audience to explore a true story about my life while showcasing my personal aesthetic and creative abilities. The project required a balance between personal storytelling and professional presentation, ensuring that the design appealed to both potential publishing houses and specific readers with interests aligned to the themes of my memoir. By investigating the conventions of book jacket design, such as typography, layout, and imagery, I aimed to create a visually compelling and genre-appropriate design that could effectively capture the attention of my intended audience.

IMAGE TYPE LAYOUT

This assignment of experimenting with type, layout, and image to create unorthodox designs played a crucial role in developing my creativity and problem-solving abilities as a designer. By stepping away from traditional design conventions, this exercise encouraged me to push boundaries and think outside the box. It challenged me to explore how unconventional design choices such as altering typography, breaking grid structures, or using unexpected image placements could still effectively communicate a message while engaging the audience in new ways.

EVENT POSTER

The purpose of this assignment was to create a visually compelling event poster tailored to a specific audience, purpose, and context. By designing a poster that communicates effectively in both digital and print environments, I learned how to analyze and address a rhetorical situation, including understanding the needs and expectations of a particular audience. The project emphasized using design elements such as typography, color, and layout to attract attention, convey essential information, and inspire action, while ensuring the design could work seamlessly across various media.

LOGOS

The purpose of this assignment was to design a logo that effectively represents a brand or organization while appealing to a specific audience and fulfilling a clear purpose. Through this project, I learned how to analyze a brand's identity, values, and target audience to create a design that communicates its essence. The assignment emphasized the importance of simplicity, scalability, and versatility in logo design, ensuring the final product would work across various media and applications, from digital platforms to print materials. By experimenting with shapes, typography, and color, I was able to create a logo that balances aesthetic appeal with functional clarity, leaving a lasting impression and strengthening the brand’s visual identity.

NON PROFIT BRANDING

The purpose of the De’ Medici Foundation’s logo was to represent the foundation’s mission and history in a clear and meaningful way. The design needed to show their commitment to helping others while honoring the artistic legacy of the Medici family. The dove was chosen to symbolize peace, hope, and renewal, reflecting the foundation’s work with disadvantaged children and global causes. The stained glass element connects to the Medici family's role as patrons of Renaissance art, representing creativity, culture, and beauty. The design process focused on combining these elements to create a logo that feels both modern and timeless. The final design captures the foundation’s dedication to both humanitarian efforts and the arts.

DIGITAL BOOK

The purpose of this project was to design a 10-page full-color digital booklet that balances aesthetics with functionality while keeping the reader engaged. This assignment introduced the challenges of multi-page design, such as creating a cohesive layout, organizing content effectively, and maintaining consistency throughout. The booklet needed to include essential elements like a front cover, table of contents, body content, and back matter, all designed to aid readability and memory.
